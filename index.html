Macro 01
Hainan (China) Free Trade Port First Quarter: Offshore Duty-Free Sales +25.7% (2026 Q1)
+25.7%
2026 Q1 offshore duty-free sales YoY growth, reaching RMB 14.21 billion (~US$2.06 billion)
  • Dec 18, 2025 — Port Closure Implemented: Full-island independent customs territory officially operational; duty-free tariff line items expanded from ~1,900 to ~6,600 (covering 74% of import/export categories)
  • 2026 New Year Holiday (3 days): Offshore duty-free sales RMB 712 million, +128.9%; 83,500 shoppers, +60.6%
  • 30% Value-Add Duty Exemption Policy (Strategic Opportunity): Jewelry brands establishing luxury packaging assembly, haute maintenance, or regional distribution hubs in Hainan can achieve full import duty exemption once local value-add reaches 30%
  • Consumer Savings Range: Watches ~20–28%, handbags ~25–30% (vs. mainland China boutiques)
Hainan's port closure is diverting a portion of China's HNWI clientele from domestic high jewelry consumption, while simultaneously creating a new venue for engaging these clients — top clients' consumption behaviors and experience preferences in Hainan merit incorporation into clientele insights.
Sources: moodiedavittreport.com · english.www.gov.cn · 18/12/2025
Macro 02
China's Two Sessions 2026 (两会): GDP Target 4.5–5%, Consumption Tax "Downstream Shift" Reform Initiated
  • Macro Setting (China): GDP growth target 4.5–5.0%; establishment of RMB 100 billion domestic demand promotion fund + RMB 250 billion "trade-in" fund
  • Consumption Tax Shift (Critical): Collection point moving from "production stage" to "retail/consumption stage," directly impacting cross-regional pricing and profit allocation models for high jewelry
  • Local Subsidy Ban: Prohibition of improper tax rebates and hidden subsidies; store expansion will rely purely on genuine sales productivity per square meter
If the consumption tax downstream shift materializes, it will directly affect high jewelry product pricing across channels, impacting top clients' price perception and purchase decisions — policy developments warrant close monitoring.

Macro 04
Deep Stratification of China's Luxury Market: HNWI Resilience vs. Structural Rise of Local Brands
  • Market Context: Bain estimates China's luxury market declined ~20% in 2024 (the largest drop since at least 2011), with stabilization signals emerging in H2 2025; Bernstein forecasts mid-single-digit percentage growth in 2026
  • Stratification Landscape: HNWIs continue to concentrate toward "investment-grade" brands (Hermès, Cartier, LV) overlaid with gold safe-haven demand; aspirational luxury consumers are accelerating migration toward local Chinese brands (Laopu Gold, etc.), with the middle segment continuing to shrink
  • Retail Confidence Signals: LV opened a landmark Beijing flagship on January 13, 2026; Tiffany and Dior opened in the same district during the same period; CNBC described this as "a strategic signal to restore consumer confidence"
  • Industry Consensus: Digital Luxury Group: "The 2026 winners will be brands that prioritize cultural insight and focus on core clients"; HSBC: A strong China recovery is not expected until 2027
The market is contracting but has not disappeared. The competitive dimension has shifted from brand awareness to "who can build the most irreplaceable emotional and asset-level connection with top-tier clientele." High jewelry exhibitions + private dinners + one-on-one client relationship management are the most effective investment areas at present.
Sources: wwd.com · 05/01/2026 · jingdaily.com · 07/04/2026 · cnbc.com · 14/01/2026
Macro 05 · Geopolitics
2026 US–Iran War (From Feb 28): Middle East Luxury Market Effectively Halted, ~$100 Billion in Global Market Cap Erased
-$100B
Global luxury stock market cap erased due to the US–Iran war (March 2026); the Middle East market (~6% of global luxury) nearly completely halted in the short term
  • War Context: On February 28, 2026, a joint US–Israeli military operation (Operation Epic Fury) killed Iranian Supreme Leader Khamenei; Iran subsequently launched massive retaliation against US bases and allied nations across the Middle East, Dubai International Airport was struck and temporarily closed, with conflict spreading to Israel, Saudi Arabia, Qatar, and the UAE
  • Direct Luxury Market Impact: Middle East market annual growth had been 6–8% (Bernstein), now nearly halted; Bernstein analyst Luca Solca: if the war ends quickly the impact is "limited," but if it persists there is recession risk
  • Energy & Supply Chain Impact: Strait of Hormuz commercial shipping disrupted, Brent crude rising to ~$120/barrel; European energy crisis (Germany and Italy facing technical recession risk), further driving up high jewelry manufacturing and logistics costs
  • Indirect Impact on China: China is one of the largest importers of Middle Eastern oil/natural gas; rising energy prices will increase domestic industrial costs, further dampening the already moderate 2026 economic growth outlook
The direct impact is relatively limited; however, the global uncertainty triggered by the war may further reinforce Chinese HNWIs' preference for "tangible assets" — this aligns with high jewelry's value-preservation narrative.
Sources: cnbc.com · 27/03/2026 · fortune.com · 20/03/2026 · britannica.com · weforum.org · 2026/03
Macro 06
Natural Diamond Crisis: Lab-Grown Diamonds Structurally Disrupting the Market, Industry Accelerating Toward "Gemstone Scarcity" Repositioning
-83%
Lab-grown diamonds ~83% cheaper than natural diamonds (1-carat lab-grown $725 vs. natural $4,200, 2026)
  • Market Landscape: Lab-grown diamonds now hold ~20% of the global diamond market; market size projected at $33.9 billion in 2026 (+7.9%); wholesale prices have dropped 90–95% since 2015
  • De Beers' Strategic Pivot: Abandoned the Lightbox lab-grown diamond business, fully committing to the "Earth Made" natural diamond narrative; opened its largest flagship at 12 Place Vendôme, Paris (directly facing Cartier), while advancing the Anglo American divestiture
  • Positive Implications for High Jewelry: The scarcity and value-preservation properties of natural large-carat colored gemstones (rubies, sapphires, emeralds, Padparadscha sapphires) are being reassessed; GIA has updated its distinction standards between lab-grown and natural diamonds, strengthening industry identification boundaries
Lab-grown diamonds' impact on the mass market is accelerating differentiation in the high jewelry category: as consumers increasingly cannot distinguish "ordinary diamond jewelry," brand narrative, the irreproducibility of rare gemstones, and the unquantifiable value of high jewelry craftsmanship become the core pillars supporting pricing and clientele selection.
Sources: debeersgroup.com · 2026/02 · leondiamond.com · 30/01/2026 · scmp.com · 19/08/2025
Macro 07
Japan Market: Weak Yen Driving Large-Scale Arbitrage, Sustained Outflow of China's HNWI Clientele
20–30%
Price differential between Japan and China on high jewelry, providing sustained incentive for China-based clients to purchase in Japan
  • Driven by sustained yen weakness, Japan's luxury market is expected to continue growing 2–3% in 2026; Tokyo is becoming a preferred destination for high-value purchases among a large number of Chinese HNWIs
  • Price differentials of up to 20–30% make purchasing Cartier high jewelry, watches, Van Cleef & Arpels, and others in Japan significantly more cost-effective than in China; this outflow directly impacts domestic per-client transaction data and CRM attribution, causing actual sales data in the China market to be understated
  • Hermès, Chanel, and other brands executed annual price adjustments in early 2026, partly aimed at narrowing regional price gaps; however, Japan remains one of the most price-competitive major luxury markets
Some top clients have already incorporated purchasing high jewelry in Japan as a routine consumption behavior; understanding this pattern helps strengthen the exclusive experience value proposition of local purchases in domestic clientele communications.
Sources: cpp-luxury.com · 2026 · pursebop.com · 2026/01
